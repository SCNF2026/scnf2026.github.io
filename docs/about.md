# About

<p class="page-lead">SCNF 2026 is conceived as a small, discussion-oriented workshop on structure and closure in nonequilibrium flows. Rather than functioning as a large conference, it is designed as a focused forum in which closely related communities can compare structural principles, isolate technical bottlenecks, and identify benchmark problems that deserve sustained joint attention.</p>

Many regimes of current interest lie beyond the classical Navier–Stokes–Fourier description: shocks, rarefied gases, high-frequency and short-scale transport, multiphase systems, and fluctuating media. In such regimes, the decisive questions are structural: which state variables to choose, which constraints a closure should satisfy, and how to preserve thermodynamic consistency together with mathematical well-posedness.

The workshop brings together perspectives from **Rational Extended Thermodynamics (RET)**, **kinetic theory and moment methods**, **GENERIC**, and related structure-preserving continuum or statistical approaches. Particular emphasis is placed on the relation between general structural principles and concrete modelling challenges in **boundary and interface conditions** and in **computational realization**.

## Main objectives

- **Compare structural principles across frameworks.**
  Clarify commonalities and differences in entropy principles, hyperbolicity, stability, reversible/irreversible coupling, and realizability.

- **Connect structural ideas to concrete nonequilibrium flow problems.**
  Discuss how closure principles affect modelling choices for shocks, rarefied gases, multiphase flow, fluctuations, interfaces, and other strongly nonequilibrium regimes.

- **Identify priorities for the next decade.**
  Distil a shortlist of open problems, shared benchmarks, and collaboration opportunities that can accelerate progress across neighboring communities.
